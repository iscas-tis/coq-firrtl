In this work, we established a general framework called Vefirrtl where programs in FIRRTL, an intermediate representation language for hardware designs, and its lowering transformations can be formally reasoned. We defined for the first time an elegant operational semantics for Low FIRRTL, a restricted form of FIRRTL programs resembling RTL Verilog. Moreover, we extract an OCaml interpreter for LoFIRRTL and validate its consistency against Treadle, the main-stream FIRRTL reference interpreter, on 120 programs collected from various sources.  Going one step further, we also formalize HiFIRRTL and its six key lowering transformations, specify and verify the functional correctness of two most involved transformations among them, namely, ``ExpandConnects'' and ``ExpandWhens''.  Vefirrtl paves the way towards a fully verified FIRRTL compiler, which is left as future work.
