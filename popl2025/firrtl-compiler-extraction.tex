Based on the formalization and correctness proofs of the lowering transformations of FIRRTL in Coq,
by utilizing the extraction mechanism available in the Coq proof assistant~\cite{Letouzey02,LET08},
we extract a verified FIRRTL compiler in OCaml. The verified OCaml compiler lowers
a given FIRRTL program by consecutively executing the three lowering transformations, namely,
``InferWidths'',
``ExpandConnects'', and ``ExpandWhens'', resulting in a {\lofirrtl} program.

To utilize our OCaml FIRRTL compiler in practice, one can replace the original FIRRTL compiler (e.g., the Scala FIRRTL compiler or
the new MLIR FIRRTL compiler) in the entire compilation tool-chain with ours. For instance,
to synthesize a circuit design in Chisel into synthesizable Verilog,
typically, high-level circuit design generators in Chisel are compiled
and executed using the convertor \textsf{ChiselStage} of the Chisel compiler framework,
resulting in FIRRTL programs, which in turn are compiled into LoFIRRTL programs by an FIRRTL compiler.
The LoFIRRTL programs are further transformed into circuits in synthesizable Verilog using, e.g.,
{\sf firtool}, a tool provided with CIRCT.

To bridge the input and output gap between our OCaml FIRRTL compiler and other tools, we develop
an FIRRTL parser and a LoFIRRTL emitter both in OCaml.
We implement the parser by defining the grammar of FIRRTL in the BNF-like form in {\tt ocamlyacc}, and specifying the
semantic-actions to produce the abstract syntax tree representation of the FIRRTL program in the OCaml types,
which can be processed by our OCaml FIRRTL compiler.
The LoFIRRTL emitter visits the abstract syntax tree representation of the LoFIRRTL program
and prints it in the MLIR syntax~\footnote{https://mlir.llvm.org} which can be processed by {\sf firtool}.
